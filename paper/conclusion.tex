\section{Conclusion and Future Work}\label{sec:conclusion}

We introduced a multi-domain CEP benchmark suite covering aviation, financial trading, and collaborative editing. Each benchmark pairs a rule set---derived from domain guidelines via a semi-automated pipeline---with a real-world stream of approximately 1.6~million events. Built on the \texttt{incubator-kie-benchmarks} infrastructure, the suite ensures reproducible performance evaluation.

The cross-suite characterisation reveals that the three domains naturally exhibit different workload profiles --- join-heavy, chain-deep, and pipeline-sequential --- providing diverse and realistic conditions for evaluating rule engine behaviour. Furthermore, high rule coverage ($>$63\%) across domains validates that the real-world streams effectively exercise the rule sets.

Future work includes scaling the rule bases to larger rule counts to study how engine performance degrades with increasing rule-base complexity, and conducting cross-engine comparisons using these benchmarks to evaluate how different production rule engines handle identical CEP workloads.

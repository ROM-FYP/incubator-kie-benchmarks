\section{Related Work}\label{sec:related}

\textbf{Rule-engine benchmarks.}
Classic workloads such as Waltz and Miss Manners~\cite{brownston1985ops5,forgy1982rete} test Rete pattern-matching on fixed synthetic data.
OpenRuleBench~\cite{liang2009openrulebench} and its successor RUBEN~\cite{angele2022ruben} broaden scope to Datalog and OWL engines but remain static.
Bobek and Misiak~\cite{bobek2017benchmarking} generate synthetic rule models parameterised by rule count.
The Train Benchmark~\cite{szarnyas2018train} adds incremental evaluation but targets graph-pattern tools, not event streams.
None incorporate temporal CEP operators or structural rule-base analysis.

\textbf{CEP benchmarks.}
LinearRoad~\cite{arasu2004linearroad}, NEXMark~\cite{tucker2003nexmark}, ESPBench~\cite{hesse2021espbench}, and Theodolite~\cite{henning2021theodolite} benchmark stream-processing throughput using synthetic streams with SQL or dataflow queries.
Wahl and Hollunder~\cite{wahl2012cep} compare CEP engines on a single event pattern; FINCoS~\cite{mendes2013fincos} provides load generation without a standard rule set.
None provide domain-derived rule sets or rule-base structural characterisation.

\textbf{The gap.}
Authoritative surveys confirm this absence: Cugola and Margara~\cite{cugola2012processing} find no benchmark exercising both rule semantics and streams; Dayarathna and Perera~\cite{dayarathna2018event} note \emph{``few widely agreed-upon benchmarks for EP''}; Bizarro et al.~\cite{bizarro2011bicep} observe \emph{``no agreed-upon benchmarks in the CEP community.''}
Our suite addresses this gap with real-world multi-domain data, rule sets derived from domain standards, temporal CEP constructs, and an empirical cross-suite characterisation.
